History of The Taj Mahal

Agra, located in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh, India, is steeped in history and is primarily known for the iconic Taj Mahal. Here's a brief historical overview:

Early History: Agra's history dates back to ancient times, with mentions in the Mahabharata, one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India. It was known as Agravana in the Mahabharata era.

Medieval Period: Agra rose to prominence during the medieval period, particularly under the Mughal Empire. In 1526, Babur, the first Mughal emperor, defeated the Sultan of Delhi, Ibrahim Lodi, in the First Battle of Panipat and established the Mughal rule in India. Agra became an important center of governance and culture during the Mughal era.

Mughal Architecture: The most famous monument of Agra, the Taj Mahal, was commissioned by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth in 1631. Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653. It is considered one of the greatest examples of Mughal architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Agra Fort: Another notable monument in Agra is the Agra Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638 when the capital was shifted to Delhi. It was originally built by Emperor Akbar in 1565 and later renovated and expanded by subsequent Mughal rulers.

Later History: Agra continued to be an important city even after the decline of the Mughal Empire. It came under the control of various rulers, including the Marathas and the British, during different periods of history.

Modern Times: Today, Agra is a major tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors from around the world to witness its rich history and architectural marvels, particularly the Taj Mahal.
